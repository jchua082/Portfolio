Redesigning the NTU Portal

UX / UI DESIGN

Year:

Skills:
2020 UX Design, Figma

Project Scope

Create a high-fidelity prototype to revamp the existing NTU website.

Initial Analysis

There are 2 main portals that NTU students can access online academic resources: iNTU and the campus website.

The iNTU portal was created to provide a one-stop access experience for NTU students, faculty and staff but often directs users to the campus website rather than displaying the information on the interface itself.

As NTU students, we find it difficult to find the resources we wanted seamlessly. Hence, we decided to redesign the NTU Portal for our project to make it more intuitive and convenient for users to navigate the site.

User Research

Our team conducted a survey with 30 NTU students to find out about our their needs and pain points using the NTU portal.

We first gathered the most popular and common type of resources NTU students use on the regular. The top 3 resources include:

  • Degree Audit
  • STARS
  • Class Schedule
Then, we created user journey maps to discover how NTU students access and engage their academic resources. We also gathered factors of the interface that users value, satisfactions levels, and their pain points using the current NTU portal.

Ideation & Prototyping

From the user research results, we identified 3 main problems with the existing NTU portal which are:

  • Tough navigation of the website with redundant information
  • Multiple unintuitive logins required
  • Disorganised links and repeated platforms
From there, we came out with 3 low-fidelity prototypes on Figma tackling these pain points. Solutions include removing unnecessary information and customizability. We also streamlined the sitemap to make the site more intuitive. To instil familiarity within the site for existing users, we utilised NTU’s primary and secondary colour scheme and typeface in our prototypes.

Repeat the Process

After creating our mock-ups, we gathered continuous feedback from our users to find out if our initial prototypes did solve their pain points and gathered areas of improvement. Some layouts were changed and new features were included to ensure that our goals of meeting users' needs were met.

Through this iterative fine-tuning process, our team successfully created our final prototype to enhance the current NTU portal for a better user experience for our target audience.
